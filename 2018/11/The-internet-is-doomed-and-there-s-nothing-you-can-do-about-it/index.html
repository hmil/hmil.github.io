The internet is doomed and there's nothing you can do about it

What makes the internet unique? Is it its ability to transfer information across the world? No, the telephone did that, the television, the radio even are all ways to transfer information over long distances. You might think the telephone is analog voice data, it isn’t as versatile as a digital channel. Well, digital TV has been around for a while, either by cable or satellite it, a digital communication channel isn’t new. How about directionnality? TV is just an input channel whereas the internet is input-output. You are getting closer but you aren’t there yet. I could still argue that (TODO: compelling argument).

Alright, let me point you to a satisfactory answer: Decentralization. Even if you aren’t a techie (which seems surprising given you are reading this blog) you have probably heard this term before. We say that the internet is decentralized because there isn’t a single place to get your stuff. You can still be using the internet exclusively as an input channel and benefit from the fact it is decentralized.

There has been quite some turmoil in US news these past years because of net neutrality. And for good reasons: defenders of the net neutrality feared that specific laws could give a select few disproportionate power (control) over the internet. But, if we try to get to the bottom of it: what makes turning the internet into an oligopoly bad? After all, the TV started out as a monopoly and is still an oligopoly today. The answer is simple: decentralization can only live as long as net neutrality does. A menace to net neutrality is a menace to decentralization.

If an actor on the internet gets too much power, its easy for that actor to force a specific supplier onto the consumers. That supplier then has an unfair advantage over the competition and ends up being the majoritary content supplier. Exit decentralization.

But what if the debate on net neutrality was a strawman which steered people away from the underlying, core issue? What if, neutral or not, the net was doomed, not because of the evil greed of a few, but because of immutable laws of nature?
